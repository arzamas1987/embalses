## How availability was checked (method & limits)
- **`.com` / `.dev` / `.app`**: queried **authoritative RDAP** servers
  (`rdap.verisign.com` for `.com`; `www.registry.google` for Google's `.dev` /
  `.app`). **HTTP 404 = no registration record found → AVAILABLE; HTTP 200 =
  REGISTERED.** This is a reliable signal.
- **`.es`**: the `.es` registry (Red.es / Dominios.es) has **no public RDAP/WHOIS
  API** reachable from here, so `.es` was probed by **authoritative NS-record
  presence** via DNS. **No NS / NXDOMAIN → "likely AVAILABLE"; NS present →
  registered/parked.** This is a *weaker* signal: a registered-but-unconfigured
  `.es` domain would also show no NS. **Always re-verify `.es` at a registrar.**
- Earlier DNS A-record checks produced false positives (matched the resolver's
  own address) and were discarded; results below rely on RDAP + NS only.

## SEO notes (per concept)
- **Descriptive Spanish roots win locally.** "embalse(s)" is the exact term
  Spanish users search; "nivel" + "agua", "reserva hídrica" are high-intent
  queries. Descriptive names rank and convert well for a Spanish audience.
- **Trade-off:** very generic descriptive names (`nivelagua`, `reservahidrica`,
  `hidromapa`) are weaker as defensible *brands/trademarks* (harder to protect,
  more lookalikes) even though they're SEO-friendly.
- **`embalsia` / `embalseo`** are coined/brandable: strong trademark potential,
  fewer collisions, and **`embalsia` doubles as "embalse + IA"** (IA = AI in
  Spanish) — on-message for the NL-assistant differentiator.
- Prefer a **`.es`** primary for a Spanish-market product (local trust + SEO
  signal), with **`.com`** secured defensively, and **`.dev`/`.app`** for
  docs/console (`api.<brand>.dev`, `app.<brand>.app`).
- Avoid hyphens and numerals; keep ≤ 11 chars where possible for recall.

## Trademark / confusion risk notes
- **Avoid confusion with existing reservoir sites**: `estadoembalses.es`,
  `embalses.net`, `embalses.info`, `snowy.es`. Do **not** pick names that read as
  a variant of "embalses.*" used by those sites. `embalsia`/`embalseo` are
  sufficiently distinct; `embalsemap` is closer to the generic "embalses" cluster
  (more confusion/coexistence risk).
- **"Acua/Aqua/Hidro/Agua" are heavily used** across water/utility brands → higher
  likelihood of prior marks; clearance search recommended before filing.
- **No trademark search was performed here.** Before committing, run a clearance
  search in the **OEPM** (Oficina Española de Patentes y Marcas) and **EUIPO**
  databases for the chosen name in the relevant classes (e.g. 9 software, 42
  SaaS, 35/38 data services).
- Coined names (`embalsia`, `embalseo`) are the most defensible as registrable
  trademarks; generic descriptive names may face distinctiveness objections.
